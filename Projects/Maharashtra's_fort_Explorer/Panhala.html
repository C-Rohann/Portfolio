Panhala Fort

Historical Significance

Panhala Fort is one of the largest forts in the Deccan and has immense historical value. It was once the capital of the Maratha Empire under Shivaji Maharaj for over a year. The fort was a major center during various battles against the Bijapur Sultanate and later the Mughals. The Battle of Pavan Khind, an epic escape led by Baji Prabhu Deshpande, is closely associated with Panhala Fort.

Architectural Features

  • Andhar Bavadi: A hidden well used as a water source and escape route.
  • Teen Darwaza & Rajwada: Grand entrance gates and royal residences within the fort.
  • Massive Perimeter: Encloses over 15 km of land, making it one of the largest forts in Maharashtra.
  • Granaries: Ancient storehouses to sustain long sieges.

Specifications of Panhala Fort

  • Height: Approx. 3,170 feet (966 meters)
  • Location: Near Kolhapur, Maharashtra
  • Perimeter: Around 15 km in circumference
  • Best Time to Visit: Post-monsoon and winter months
