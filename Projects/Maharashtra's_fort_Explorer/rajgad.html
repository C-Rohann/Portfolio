Rajgad Fort

Historical Significance

Rajgad Fort served as the first capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It remained the seat of Maratha power for more than 25 years before the capital was moved to Raigad. The fort witnessed numerous significant events in Shivaji’s life, including the birth of his son Rajaram and the death of his wife Saibai.

Architectural Features

  • Balekilla (Citadel): The highest point of the fort, offering panoramic views and strong defense.
  • Sanjivani Machi: A long fortified spur providing extra defense.
  • Padmavati Temple: A small temple used for worship and rest by Shivaji.
  • Palaces & Water Cisterns: Remains of palaces and strategically located water storage systems.

Specifications of Rajgad Fort

  • Height: 4,514 feet (1,376 meters)
  • Location: 60 km southwest of Pune
  • Built: In the 14th century, but expanded by Shivaji Maharaj in 1648
  • Material: Stone and lime

Importance of Rajgad Fort

The fort was not only a military base but also a symbol of the growing Maratha power. Major decisions and key campaigns were launched from this stronghold. Its strategic location and elevation made it nearly impregnable at the time.

Even today, trekkers and history enthusiasts consider Rajgad one of the most rewarding and sacred climbs.

Helpful Note

Rajgad Fort is ideal for trekking and night camping. It’s advisable to begin the trek early in the morning and carry sufficient water. Local villagers often provide food and guidance. The view from Balekilla during sunrise is breathtaking and not to be missed!
