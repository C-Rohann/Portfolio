Torna Fort

Historical Significance

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandgad, is the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1646 at the young age of 16. This marked the beginning of the Maratha Empire. The fort's capture symbolized the strategic brilliance and vision of Shivaji Maharaj and holds a special place in Maratha history.

Architectural Features

  • Zunjar Machi and Budhla Machi: Two massive bastions providing commanding views and defensive strength.
  • Large Fortification Walls: Built to withstand sieges and enemy attacks.
  • Temples: A temple dedicated to goddess Mengai and several ancient structures.
  • Rock Cut Water Tanks: Reliable sources of water for the fort inhabitants.

Specifications of Torna Fort

  • Height: 4,603 feet (Highest fort in Pune district)
  • Location: Velhe Taluka, Pune District
  • Other Name: Prachandgad ("Massive Fort")
  • Base Village: Velhe

Importance of Torna Fort

Torna Fort's capture was a pivotal moment in Maratha history and demonstrated the ambition and tactical ability of young Shivaji. Its commanding position made it an essential fortification in the southwest of the Maratha territory and served as a foundation for future conquests.
