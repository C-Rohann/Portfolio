About Maratha History

The Maratha Empire, founded in the 17th century by the visionary Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, was known for its remarkable military strategies, administrative skills, and the spirit of swarajya (self-rule). The Marathas established a strong and decentralized governance system that empowered local communities while challenging foreign rulers like the Mughals and the British.

Shivaji Maharaj strategically built and reinforced forts like Raigad, Rajgad, Pratapgad, Torna, and Sindhudurg, not only as military bases but also as centers of administration. These forts were often built atop hills and cliffs, taking full advantage of the terrain for defense.

After Shivaji Maharaj, his son Chhatrapati Sambhaji Maharaj courageously defended the empire against the Mughal invasion. Despite immense pressure, he upheld the ideals of the Maratha legacy until his tragic martyrdom.

The Peshwas, led initially by Balaji Vishwanath and then famously by Bajirao I, carried the Maratha influence far into North India. They played a crucial role in expanding the empire and maintaining its administrative and military strength. Their contributions made Pune a cultural and political hub during the 18th century.

Today, these forts and their stories stand as proud reminders of the bravery, resilience, and strategic genius of the Maratha rulers.
