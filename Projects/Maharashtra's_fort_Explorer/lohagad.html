Lohgad Fort

Historical Significance

Lohgad Fort, meaning "Iron Fort," was one of the prominent forts used by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj to store treasury looted during military campaigns. Due to its strong natural defense and elevated position, it played an important role in controlling the trade route to Konkan.

Architectural Features

  • Vinchu Kata: A long, narrow fortified spur that looks like a scorpion’s tail, ideal for defense and surveillance.
  • Four Massive Gates: Ganesh Darwaja, Narayan Darwaja, Hanuman Darwaja, and Maha Darwaja.
  • Rock Cut Water Cisterns: Still used by trekkers and local travelers.
  • Stone Walls & Bastions: Strong construction built to withstand invasion and time.

Specifications of Lohgad Fort

  • Height: Approx. 3,400 feet
  • Location: Near Lonavala, in Pune District
  • Best Season: Monsoon and post-monsoon for lush green views
  • Connected Fort: Visapur Fort is nearby and often visited together

Importance of Lohgad Fort

The fort served as a strategic point for safeguarding the empire’s treasures and played a vital role in guarding routes to the western coast. It is one of the best-preserved forts and is still a favorite for beginner trekkers due to its ease of access and breathtaking views.

Helpful Note

Lohgad is ideal for a one-day trek. During the monsoon, the trail is covered with mist, moss, and waterfalls. Wear trekking shoes with good grip, and don't miss the panoramic view from Vinchu Kata.
