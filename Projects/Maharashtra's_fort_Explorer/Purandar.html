Purandar Fort

Historical Significance

Purandar Fort holds immense historical importance in the Maratha Empire. It is the birthplace of Sambhaji Maharaj, the son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort was a stronghold in battles and played a key role during the Treaty of Purandar between Shivaji Maharaj and the Mughals in 1665.

Architectural Features

  • Two Fort Sections: The fort is divided into Purandar (the main fort) and Vajragad (smaller adjacent fort).
  • Temples: Includes the Kedareshwar temple and a church built during British rule.
  • Fortification: Strong walls, gates, and observation points for defense and surveillance.
  • Panoramic Views: Offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and villages.

Specifications of Purandar Fort

  • Height: Around 4,472 feet
  • Location: Near Saswad, Pune District
  • Region: Western Ghats (Sahyadri range)
  • Structure: Dual fort (Purandar & Vajragad)

Importance of Purandar Fort

Purandar Fort has played a major role in the Maratha resistance. It served both as a military base and a symbolic site, being the birthplace of a royal heir. It also witnessed significant treaties and diplomatic events that shaped Maratha history.
